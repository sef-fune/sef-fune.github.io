Mental Health during a Pandemic

The Pandemic

The COVID-19 pandemic, an event that worsened in 2020, has been shown to affect the mental health of many, especially students and those who have lost their jobs.

What is mental health? How have we been able to somehow manage our own mental health during these times? What are the experiences of people who have dealt with possible mental health problems?

What is Mental Health?

Mental health refers to a person's emotional, psychological, and social well-being. Our mental health affects our day-to-day actions and, like physical health, problems can arise due to it. (mentalhealth.gov, n.d.)

Why is Mental Health important?

Mental health is important because of how much it affects us. One's mood and behavior can easily be affected by a sudden change in a person's mental health. There are many factors that can contribute to these issues, such as possible trauma from past experiences or heretidary mental health problems. (mentalhealth.gov, n.d.)

Changes and Problems regarding Mental Health

Some examples of mental health problems include depression, anxiety problems, and eating disorders. Mental health problems can cause a person to have severe changes in their lifestyle. They might start socializing less, feeling hopeless, and hearing voices. There are some other clear physical signs, such as eating less, self-harm, and an inability to perform simple tasks.
